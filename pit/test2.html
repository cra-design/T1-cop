Line 48500 – Balance owing

You may be looking for:

  • Balance owing

After you report your income and claim your deductions, credits, and expenses, you can calculate whether you will have a balance owing or get a refund. You need to pay the amount that you owe to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) on or before April 30, 2025 to avoid paying interest and penalties.

Subtract your total credits from your total payable:

  Line 43500: Total payable (total income tax that you owe for the year)
-  Line 48200: Total credits (total income tax that you paid during the year, including instalment payments and refundable credits)
=  Your refund or balance owing

If the result is positive, you paid less income tax than you needed to for the year. Enter the result at line 48500 on your return. This amount is your estimated balance owing. Your notice of assessment (NOA) or notice of reassessment (NOR) will show the final result.

You have to make a payment on or before the due date if your balance owing is $2 or more.

Find payment options: How to pay your balance owing

If the result is negative, refer to: Line 48400 - Refund
